Key finding

Globally, water stress and agricultural employment do not show a simple positive correlation — many extremely water-stressed countries (Gulf states, Israel) have low agricultural employment, because they have diversified into oil, services, and finance.

The most vulnerable countries are those that combine high stress with large agricultural workforce shares — mainly in South Asia and the Sahel. India stands out: with a BWS score of 4.1 and 42% of its workforce in agriculture, it represents the largest absolute number of water-exposed agricultural jobs in the world.

The trend line tells a structural story: globally, ag. employment has declined from 32.6% (1991) to 22% (2025), even as water stress intensifies — meaning the workers who remain in agriculture are increasingly concentrated in the highest-stress contexts.
